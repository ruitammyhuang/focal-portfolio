Building Focal: A Human–AI Collaborative System Design and Development Case

Dr. Rui Tammy Huang, a Clinical Assistant Professor at the University of Florida, used Claude AI to systematically design and build a complex, multi-layer workload management system from scratch, documenting every step to generate knowledge for CS Education and Instructional Design research and practice.

About the System Built

Focal: Faculty Workload Management System

A Python/Flask web application that bridges multiple per-project Notion databases into a unified faculty workload tracker that automatically syncs tasks, logs work sessions, and surfaces prioritized work views without manual data entry.

Portfolio Purpose

Four Interlocking Goals

This portfolio is not just a record. It is a generative artifact that serves research, teaching, and design simultaneously.

01 · Knowledge
Frameworks for Human–AI System Design
Discover and build reusable knowledge, checklists, and frameworks on how humans can best use AI to systematically plan, design, and develop complex systems, going far beyond simple prompting.
02 · Competencies
What Skills This Reveals We Need to Teach
Analyze the process to identify the competencies demonstrated (what to amplify) and the competencies the process revealed as missing or difficult (what to design curriculum around).
03 · Design Artifacts
Iteratively Refined Design Documents
Maintain and improve the actual system design documents as exemplars, showing what rigorous AI-assisted system design documentation looks like at each stage of development.
04 · Lessons Learned
What Works and What to Avoid
Build a growing repository of lessons (both successes and pitfalls) so educators can teach students what actually happens when you try to build something real with AI assistance.

About

Who Built This

Dr. Rui Tammy Huang is a Clinical Assistant Professor of Computer Science Education and Educational Technology at the University of Florida. This project emerged from a practical need: managing a multi-faceted academic job (teaching, research, program management, professional service, and more) without over laboring the workload.

Rather than purchasing a tool, Tammy used Claude AI as a collaborative partner to design and build a custom system, treating the process itself as a reflexive case study for understanding what human–AI collaboration on complex, real-world system design actually looks like.
